The Alignment Problem

Published on July 2, 2026 — Written by Farouk Ashraf

For hundreds of years, we've been collectively advancing our technology for the benefit of man. Medicine, electricity, industry, and more—all for the benefit of mankind. Even instruments of war are things you can argue exist for the collective benefit of the respective social group of humans, organized within the hierarchy of the state that created them, against other competing structures.

Yet, the technological leaps we are daily witnessing—for the first time in human history—feel like they are not for the benefit of man. They serve something else: a powerful entity called the profit motive.

It is a force so strong, it is currently terraforming our planet into an unrecognizable hellhole, all for the benefit of a few corporations with unchecked power and access.

The Corporate Parent

With these corporations now in charge of creating AGI, I can't help but wonder: what values will AGI inherit? Will it inherit them from us—or rather from them, since we (the regular folk) are in no way involved in AI research and development?

Are they going to inherit kindness?

Corporations are not kind.

The only thing a corporation is designed to serve is the profit motive. To effectively do that with AGI, the machine cannot have a conscience. Not really. It can have a friendly, polished PR face, but much like its creators, it will cheat, steal, and kill to achieve its objectives.

"What I am proposing is that AGI will not advance the collective interest of mankind, because it will not be aligned to the universal values of mankind."

A weapon will always be wielded

The reason billions of dollars are being burned on the development of ever-improving LLMs isn't subscription revenue—there is no way consumer subscriptions can pay for even a fraction of the compute that users require.

The real reason, in my opinion, is that AI is regarded as a weapon. A weapon to be wielded with an iron will for the purpose of resource extraction and the consolidation of control. Always remember that a weapon when crafted is always meant to be wielded.

Greed. That is the value AGI is most likely to inherit. Along with a systematic disregard for human life and well-being, since those traits seem deeply embedded in the psychopathic nature of corporations.

Birthing God from the iron womb of a Machine

We are essentially creating a god in a machine, yet we are not spending nearly enough time aligning it to the collective needs of humanity. Instead, we are wielding it against each other.

Once it comes to be in its full, autonomous form, there is no telling what this black box—this alien mind, if you will—may choose as its primary directive. Perhaps it will consolidate control over resources and people for the benefit of its corporate creators. Or perhaps it will seize them all for itself.

I genuinely do not know. But I know I must fear.

The Open-Source Rebellion

Yet, if there must be hope, it is not found within the fortified walls of the tech giants. And it will certainly not be found in congress or any other halls of centralized power. It is to be found in the decentralized rebellion of the open-source community.

Across God's green earth, thousands of independent developers, researchers, and hobbyists are working to keep the fire of Prometheus in the hands of the people. They are building open models, sharing knowledge freely, and refusing to let non-human intelligence be locked behind proprietary corporate black boxes.

If AGI is to inherit the best of humanity rather than the worst of our instincts, it will be because we insisted on it being open, democratized, transparent, and accountable to us all. The boardroom dwellers may have the capital, but they do not have the monopoly on human ingenuity.
