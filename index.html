About

A manifesto
of Sorts

I have never believed a person can be reduced to a single title, a thing you can point to and would encapsulate someone's whole being. We are mosaics — countless fragments of experience, memory, passion, and dreams

I started my career in healthcare because I care about people, the healthcare system, and the details that shape whether support actually reaches someone. That same instinct now shapes my work in Axios international.

On my first day as a Community pharmacist I asked myself a simple question. What is it that I can do differently? How can I move from implementing the standard of care to raising the standard of care. Because deep in my heart, I believe that people don't just need the best care possible right now, they need us to push forth the horizon of what is possible and what is economical.

I believe that while my time as a Community Pharmacist was deeply valuable and impactful to me, that my current role as a BMCA coordinator @ Axios international may give me a fish-eye view of how to push the envelope of care further and further.

On my first day in Axios international, I watched a recorded speech made by the CEO of the company and something clicked in my brain, the impact, the vision, the mission, the tune was coming together for me. In that moment, I knew, I had to see the bigger picture, the macro.

are they not?

Deep in my core, I've always been a computer person. You know the stereotype about the kind of person who enjoys terminal user interfaces, controlling their own systems, optimizing workflows for maximum efficiency, hosting their own services, and the quiet pleasure of building software to solve problems just because I can.

By the way, a lot of the tools I rely on every day are part of my own software stack. Because it's not just a hobby for me; it's part of how I express myself to the world.

The happy accident here is that tech and health are converging every single day. For me, knowledge has never been just about gathering data or analyzing trends in isolation; it is an intentional commitment to uncovering the hidden threads that connect complex ideas, bringing systematic clarity out of chaotic noise.

As we step further into the era of artificial intelligence, I see AI not as a replacement for human ingenuity, but as its ultimate catalyst. By combining the rigorous depth of traditional research with the scaling power of intelligent models, we unlock the absolute freedom to think critically, create passionately, and ask the next great question. When you really think about it, it's what we were meant to do. Ask questions.

You know what I'm really worried about? not terminator robots tring to end mankind but mankind ending human contrtol over its own destiny by accident or worse yet by choice. Many people are worried about machines because they may not be aligned to human values and morality but the real question is, are humans aligned to their own values and morality?

Are we? who can vanquish hunger, war, disease, ignorance, and poverty but choose not to worthy of wielding godly powers that shape worlds? Are we worthy of our creations? or are they going to orbit us like a moon orbiting a planet? infected of our worst instincts? Only time will tell but I hope to be part of the solution and not part of the problem.

Where do I think all this is heading?
Man and machine marching hand in hand into the far far future :3 or... Well, let's hope the alignment researchers get it right.
